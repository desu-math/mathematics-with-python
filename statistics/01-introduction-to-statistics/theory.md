# Introduction to Statistics

## Overview

Statistics is the branch of mathematics concerned with **collecting, organizing, analyzing, interpreting, and presenting data**.

It provides the tools needed to transform raw data into meaningful information, enabling informed decisions and reliable conclusions.

In modern science, engineering, business, medicine, economics, and artificial intelligence, statistics plays a fundamental role in understanding data and uncertainty.

---

# Why Study Statistics?

Data is generated everywhere—from scientific experiments and business transactions to social media and sensor networks.

Without statistical methods, large amounts of data are difficult to understand or interpret.

Statistics helps us:

* summarize large datasets,
* identify patterns and trends,
* measure variability,
* make predictions,
* test hypotheses,
* support decision-making using evidence.

---

# Statistics in Data Science

Statistics is one of the core foundations of data science.

It provides the mathematical tools required to:

* explore datasets,
* identify relationships between variables,
* estimate unknown quantities,
* evaluate machine learning models,
* quantify uncertainty,
* draw reliable conclusions from data.

Many machine learning algorithms are built upon statistical concepts.

---

# Branches of Statistics

Statistics is generally divided into two major branches.

## 1. Descriptive Statistics

Descriptive statistics focuses on **summarizing and describing data**.

Common tasks include:

* organizing data,
* calculating averages,
* measuring variability,
* creating tables and charts,
* visualizing distributions.

The objective is to describe the characteristics of a dataset without making conclusions beyond the observed data.

---

## 2. Inferential Statistics

Inferential statistics uses **sample data to make conclusions about a larger population**.

It includes:

* estimation,
* confidence intervals,
* hypothesis testing,
* prediction,
* statistical modeling.

Because studying an entire population is often impractical, inferential statistics allows us to draw conclusions from carefully selected samples.

---

# Population and Sample

A **population** is the complete collection of individuals, objects, or observations of interest.

Examples:

* all students in a university,
* all households in a city,
* all registered vehicles in a country.

A **sample** is a subset selected from the population for analysis.

The goal of sampling is to obtain information about the population without observing every member.

---

# Parameter and Statistic

A **parameter** is a numerical characteristic of a population.

Examples include:

* population mean,
* population variance,
* population proportion.

A **statistic** is a numerical characteristic computed from a sample.

Statistics are used to estimate the corresponding population parameters.

---

# Statistical Workflow

A typical statistical study follows these steps:

```text
Define the problem
        ↓
Collect data
        ↓
Organize data
        ↓
Analyze data
        ↓
Interpret results
        ↓
Make conclusions
```

This workflow forms the basis of most data analysis projects.

* It involves collecting, organizing, analyzing, interpreting, and presenting data.
